ChatGPT: "I can generate images to illustrate human culture and industry. Let's start with one depicting a bustling city, a symbol of human industrial advancement, and cultural diversity."


By Masashi Ijichi

Generative AI is changing our lives. Not long ago, many people used google search to look things up, but consumers and companies alike are enamored with the convenience of being able to get answers faster and more intuitively.

But are there any pitfalls in this? We will explore through Q&As between ChatGPT and alien, who is eager to learn about the earth. The alien seems excited to know that it can use the Earth-originated ChatGPT.

Who are the main players in the race to develop generative AI?


The Q&A with aliens showed that chatgpt is still under development. However, according to Bloomberg, US-based OpenAI, the operator of ChatGPT is expected to earn 3.4billion dollars this year. As the perfection of generative AI increases, sales revenues are likely to increase further.


According to Emerging Technology Observatory, the U.S. also leads in the number of AI-related patents, ahead of China. Chinese companies, including Bytedance and Huawei, are also devoting significant resources to the new technology. The gap with the third place and below is large, and it seems that people around the world will continue to rely on generative AI originating from these two super powers.
